Lesson 2: Past Perfect

Lesson Overview

Content: learning how to use the past perfect tense in English.

In this lesson, you will be told specifically when to use the past perfect tense, and you will practice forming sentences using this tense.

Grammar Overview

What is the past perfect tense?

The past perfect tense is used to describe an action that was completed before another action took place in the past. It is formed using “had” followed by the past participle of the verb.

Example: I had finished my homework before I went to school.

Let issus the sentence above, if I break it down according to the sentence action are following the below table:

Order Action
1st I had finished my homework.
2nd I went to school.

Note: Past Perfect is one way to tell the order of actions that happened in the past. Therefore, that means you do not always need a past perfect (all the time) to tell it. You can also use other ways to do it as well, like using prepositions (before, until, previous..) to tell the order.

How? I finished my homework before I went to school.

When to use the past perfect tense?

  1. To show that an action was completed before another action in the past.
    • Example:
      • They had spoken to her before she left the office.

How to form the past perfect tense?

The past perfect tense is formed using the auxiliary verb “had” followed by the past participle of the main verb.

Formula: Subject + had + past participle of the verb

  • Example Sentences:
    • I had eaten breakfast.
    • She had finished her homework.
    • They had left the party.

Ohhh, How about Negative Sentences and Questions?

(-): Negative Sentences

To form negative sentences in the past perfect tense, you simply add “not” after “had”.

Formula: Subject + had + not + past participle of the verb

  • Example Sentences:
    • I had not eaten breakfast.
    • She had not finished her homework.
    • They had not left the party.

(?): Questions

To form questions in the past perfect tense, you invert the subject and “had”.

Formula: Had + subject + past participle of the verb?

  • Example Sentences:
    • Had you eaten breakfast?
    • Had she finished her homework?
    • Had they left the party?
